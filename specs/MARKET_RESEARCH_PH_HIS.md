# Market Research: Hospital Information System (HIS) in the Philippines (2024-2025)

## 1. Executive Summary
The Philippine digital health market is undergoing a rapid transformation, driven by the **Universal Health Care (UHC) Act of 2019** and the **Philippine Digital Health Strategy 2024–2028**. The shift from paper-based records to integrated digital systems is no longer optional but a regulatory requirement for licensing and reimbursement.

## 2. Market Size and Growth
*   **Market Value:** The broader e-health market (HIS, EMR, Telemedicine) reached approximately **USD 2.8 billion in 2024** and is projected to reach **USD 3.1 billion by 2025**.
*   **Growth Rate:** The HIS sub-market specifically is expected to grow at a **CAGR of 14.1%** through 2030.
*   **Regional Focus:** **Metro Manila (NCR)** remains the largest market (45% revenue), but **Cebu** and **Davao** are the fastest-growing provincial hubs.

## 3. Regulatory Framework & Compliance
Compliance is the highest barrier to entry in the Philippine market. Any HIS must satisfy three main entities:

### A. Department of Health (DOH)
*   **Licensing:** A validated EMR/HIS is a prerequisite for the renewal of a hospital's **License to Operate (LTO)**.
*   **Standards:** Must follow the **National Health Data Reporting Category Standards (NHDRCS)** and use **ICD-10** coding.
*   **Reporting:** Mandatory data uploading to the **National Health Data Repository (NHDR)**.

### B. PhilHealth (eClaims)
*   **Accreditation:** Only HIS solutions certified as a **Health Information Technology Provider (HITP)** can transmit claims.
*   **eClaims 3.0:** Latest standards require support for:
    *   **eSOA:** Electronic Statement of Account.
    *   **DRG:** Diagnosis Related Group shadow billing.
    *   **eGov Integration:** QR code scanning for patient admission via the eGov Super App.

### C. Data Privacy Act (RA 10173)
*   Strict requirements for data encryption, role-based access control (RBAC), and detailed audit trails.

## 5. Major Trends for 2025
*   **Cloud-First Adoption:** ~60% of the market is shifting toward cloud-based HIS to lower upfront CAPEX.
*   **AI & Predictive Analytics:** Moving from record-keeping to using AI for predictive patient outcomes and automated billing accuracy.
*   **Interoperability (HL7 FHIR):** Transitioning toward the **HL7 FHIR** standard to enable seamless data exchange across the national health network.
*   **Telemedicine Integration:** Hybrid care models are now standard; HIS platforms must include integrated video consultation and remote monitoring modules.

## 6. Strategic Recommendations
1.  **Focus on "PhilHealth-First":** Any system that simplifies the complex eClaims process has a significant competitive advantage.
2.  **SaaS for Mid-Sized Clinics:** While large hospitals are saturated, there is a gap for affordable, compliant SaaS solutions for secondary hospitals and multi-specialty clinics.
3.  **Offline-Sync Capability:** Given internet stability issues in provincial areas, a "cloud-first but offline-capable" architecture is highly desirable.
